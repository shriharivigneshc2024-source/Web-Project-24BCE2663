JPMorgan Chase & Co.

JPMorgan Chase & Co. is an American multinational financial services company and the largest bank in the United States as well as the world’s largest bank by market capitalization. Headquartered in New York City, it traces its roots back over 225 years through predecessors such as J.P. Morgan & Co. and Chase Manhattan Bank. Formed in its current structure in 2000 via the merger of J.P. Morgan & Co. and Chase Manhattan, the firm operates globally through the J.P. Morgan and Chase brands, offering investment banking, commercial banking, asset and wealth management, retail banking, and credit card services. Led by chairman and CEO Jamie Dimon, JPMorgan Chase plays a major role in global finance and is considered systemically important due to its size, worldwide presence, and influence.
